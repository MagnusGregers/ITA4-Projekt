CO₂ from Transport – What’s the Deal?

Ever wondered how much pollution our cars, buses, trains, and planes pump into the air? This page from Our World in Data breaks it down – per person, per country.

So, what’s CO₂ from transport?

It’s the carbon dioxide (CO₂) released when we travel. Scientists measure how much each person "contributes" by dividing a country’s transport emissions by its population. Spoiler: some countries are way worse than others! Flying between countries and giant ships crossing oceans – those are counted elsewhere.

Why the big difference?

Some places love cars and long road trips (hello, high emissions). Others rock public transport and clean energy – way better for the planet.

Then why should we care?

Because knowing who pollutes and how helps us find smarter, greener ways to get around – like biking, taking the bus, or going electric
